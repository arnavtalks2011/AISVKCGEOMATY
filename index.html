Millets: The Future of Sustainable Food

Introduction to Millets

Millets are a group of highly variable small-seeded grasses, widely grown around the world as cereal crops or grains for fodder and human food. They are drought-resistant and can be cultivated in semi-arid regions, making them an ideal crop for sustainable agriculture.

Types of Millets Found in India

Map showcasing the distribution of different types of millets across India.

Foxtail Millet (Kakum/Kangni)

Foxtail millet is known for its good storage properties and can be stored for a long time without getting infested with insects. It is rich in dietary fiber and minerals.

Finger Millet (Ragi)

Finger millet, also known as Ragi, is rich in calcium and is considered good for bones and overall health. It is widely used in South Indian cuisine.

Proso Millet (Cheena)

Proso millet is high in protein and gluten-free, making it an excellent choice for people with gluten intolerance. It has a low glycemic index, beneficial for diabetics.

Barnyard Millet (Sanwa)

Barnyard millet is known for its high fiber content and is often used as a healthy rice substitute. It is also rich in iron and helps in reducing cholesterol levels.

Kodo Millet (Kodra)

Kodo millet is high in fiber and antioxidants, making it beneficial for managing diabetes and cardiovascular diseases. It also aids in weight loss and digestion.

Little Millet (Kutki)

Little millet is a rich source of B vitamins, essential for various metabolic functions in the body. It is also a good source of minerals like calcium, iron, and zinc.

Bajra (Pearl Millet)

Pearl millet, commonly known as Bajra, is widely grown in India. It is rich in protein, fiber, and essential minerals like magnesium and iron. It is particularly beneficial in controlling diabetes and heart health.

Sorghum (Jowar)

Sorghum, also known as Jowar, is a nutrient-rich grain that is high in protein, iron, and fiber. It is gluten-free and helps in managing cholesterol and weight.
